Transportation Engineering Discipline was introduced in the Department of Civil Engineering, National Institute of Technology, Warangal, in 1968 under the aegis of UNESCO-UNDP and the Government of India, in the realization of the emerging area and the urgent need to train Engineers/Planners in this discipline. This is the first Institution in India to have started a full-fledged M.Tech Degree Programme in Transportation Engineering. Under the able guidance of Prof. Martin Ekse of Washington State University, USA, Prof. V.V. Sylyanov of Moscow Automobile and Road Construction Institute, USSR, and other distinguished experts in India, the Transportation Engineering discipline has not only emerged as a forerunner in the country but also created competent Transportation Engineering cadre in the country, who are now serving in various National and International bodies. With the support of UNESCO-UNDP and the Government of India, basic infrastructure in the emerging area of Transportation Engineering has been created for over three decades. This includes computational facilities in the form of Personal Computers, Video equipment, Pavement and Material testing equipment, and a Traffic data acquisition system. In realization of the excellent work done by the group in training the students for the M.Tech and Doctoral degrees in Transportation Engineering, in-service training under continuing Education Programmes, Research and Development activities and Consultation jobs, the Ministry of Education (MoE) erstwhile Ministry of Human Resources Development (MHRD), Government of India has established a "Centre for Transportation Engineering" and provided additional inputs for the modernization of laboratories and other infrastructure facilities. It now has, Automatic Traffic Analysers and Pavement Testing Equipment apart from all traditional testing facilities. The Transportation Engineering Division is now identified as one of the Canters of Excellence in Urban Transport by the Ministry of Urban Development under the 11th Five Year Plan. NRRDA has recognized the Transportation Division, Ministry of Rural Development, GOI as one of the State Technical Agency (STA) and Principal Technical Agency (PTA) for the state of Telangana and three districts in Andhra Pradesh to provide technical advice to and to train the Panchayat Raj Engineers on planning, design, construction and maintenance of rural roads under PMGSY scheme. Besides teaching, the faculty members are actively involved in state-of-the-art research, consultancy, and continuing education programs. Students and scholars work very closely with the faculty members and are primary contributors to the research and consultancy activities in the Division. The Division, with its varied faculty expertise, offers courses and conducts research in three broad areas: Traffic Engineering, Transportation Planning, and Pavement Engineering.
